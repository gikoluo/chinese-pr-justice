I am a first-generation graduate student — the first in my family to pursue an advanced degree — and I want to share how prolonged security screening is putting everything I have worked for at risk.

## My Timeline

I submitted my study permit application on November 9, 2025. Just ten days later, on November 19, 2025, it was referred to security screening. On November 21, 2025, I submitted a Privacy Act request (ATIP) to CSIS simply to find out the status of my own file.

As of today, my application has sat in total stagnation under security screening for over 240 days. My ATIP request has fared no better: CSIS has unlawfully withheld my personal information for more than 230 days past its statutory deadline, stripping me of my basic right to know what is happening to my own case.

Left with no practical recourse, I commenced a Writ of Mandamus in the Federal Court of Canada and have already fully filed my Reply memorandum. I should not have to sue my own government just to get an answer.

## Impact on My Academic Career and Family

Without a privileged background or a pre-paved road, every milestone in my academic path has been earned through countless sleepless nights and complete devotion to my research. I recently completed my Master's degree and broke through systemic barriers to earn a fully-funded Ph.D. offer in Civil Engineering at the University of British Columbia's Okanagan campus, with a nomination for the federal Canada Impact+ Research Training Award (CIRTA). My program start date is firmly set for September 1, 2026.

This delay now threatens the total forfeiture of my Ph.D. position, my funding, and my research award nomination. When I received my unconditional offer of admission, my family and I believed that a lifetime of sacrifice had finally paid off. Instead, that hard-won milestone has been turned into an agonizing crisis by an opaque bureaucratic process. The endless silence has been emotionally devastating for me and for the family who sacrificed so much to help me get here.

My doctoral research is strictly civilian and non-sensitive — it focuses on green building technologies, carbon reduction, and climate resilience. There is no plausible safety concern in my file. All this delay does is arbitrarily paralyze a young scholar trying to contribute to global sustainability, while my September 1, 2026 enrollment deadline draws closer every day.

## What I Want the Government to Know

We are not just case numbers. Behind every pending application is a real human being whose life, career, and family are being actively dismantled by indefinite administrative silence.

Conducting background checks for purely civilian, climate-focused academic research for nearly eight months — while simultaneously violating the Privacy Act by withholding my own personal records — is a severe abuse of process and a violation of basic transparency. Bureaucratic inefficiency should not be masked as "national security."

I am proud to stand with the other families sharing their stories here. We are not cold case numbers to be indefinitely set aside in a government database. We are real people, with real families, facing real, catastrophic consequences. Stop the endless bureaucratic delays. Give us our futures back.

Zenghui Liu
