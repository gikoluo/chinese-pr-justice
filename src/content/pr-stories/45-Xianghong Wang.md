# Waiting in Security Screening: A Grandparent’s Hope to Reunite with Family

I am a **parent and grandparent** whose immigration application has been stuck in **security screening**, with no clear answers from **Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC)**.

I first submitted an **Interest to Sponsor form in 2020** for the **Parents and Grandparents Sponsorship Program (PGP)**. After waiting through the lottery process, I was finally **invited to apply in October 2023**.

I submitted my **complete application on October 22, 2023**.

Since then, I have been waiting for a **final decision from IRCC**, and it has now been **29 months** without resolution. I have been informed that my application has entered **security screening**.

Earlier in the process, IRCC requested that I complete a **medical examination**, which I did immediately as required. However, after completing the medical exam, there has been **no further progress or communication** regarding my application.

My wife and I have tried to seek information through the **IRCC website**, but the responses we received did not provide any meaningful clarification about our situation.

This ongoing uncertainty has created **significant anxiety and confusion** for our family.

Our greatest wish is simply to **reunite with our only daughter**, who is currently **serving the Canadian government**, and to spend time with our **five-year-old granddaughter during her important early years**.

Being separated from our family during this time has placed **enormous emotional pressure** on us. We are eager to know **when a decision will be made**, or at the very least to receive clear information about the status of our application.

Adding to the difficulty, my **Canadian visitor visa has already expired**. If I attempt to apply for a new visitor visa, it is likely that I will once again be placed into **security screening**.

This means I may have **no practical way to enter Canada to see my family** while this process continues.

## What I Want the Government to Know

We understand and respect the importance of **national security**.

However, security screening **should not become an indefinite process**.

Applicants deserve **transparency, accountability, and a reasonable timeline** so that families like ours can plan their lives and reunite with loved ones.
