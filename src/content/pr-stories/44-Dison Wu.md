## Startup Founder Waiting in Security Screening While Building Climate Technology in Canada

### Timeline

**May 2023**
I submitted my permanent residence application through the **non-Express Entry stream** with a nomination from the **Ontario Masters Graduate Stream (OINP)**.

**July 2023**
I received my **Acknowledgement of Receipt (AOR)** from IRCC.

**December 2023**
IRCC requested additional documents, after which my application entered **security screening**.

**2024–2025**
During this period, I contacted my **Member of Parliament** multiple times to inquire about the status of my application. Each response confirmed that my application remained **under security screening**.

**2026 (Present)**
The security screening has now lasted **over two years**, and the **total processing time is approaching three years**.

---

### Building a Canadian Climate Technology Startup

In addition to my immigration application, I am also the **co-founder of a technology startup called Spero Analytics**, which was established in **2023** based on research from my **graduate studies at the University of Toronto**.

Our company works with **Canadian landfills and petrochemical companies** to help reduce **methane emissions** using our technology.

Over the past few years, our work has been recognized and supported by the Canadian government. We have received **multiple environmental innovation grants**, including funding from:

* **Natural Resources Canada (NRCan)**
* **Environment and Climate Change Canada (ECCC)**

The **total funding for these projects is close to CAD $500,000**.

---

### The Impact of Immigration Delays

My **Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP)** will expire in **May 2026**.

Because of the prolonged delays in my PR application, the **future development of our company now faces significant uncertainty**.

As the **technical lead** of the company, my immigration status directly affects our ability to continue delivering the projects we are responsible for. The delays in security screening could therefore **negatively impact government-funded innovation projects** that Canada itself has chosen to support.

---

### A Broader Impact

I hope the government understands that **prolonged security screening delays do not only affect individuals**.

They can also **undermine innovative companies, environmental technologies, and public-funded projects** that Canada has invested in and encouraged entrepreneurs to build.

For those of us who are trying to contribute to Canada’s economy and climate innovation efforts, a **timely and transparent immigration process** is essential.
