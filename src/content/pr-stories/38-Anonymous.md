**Subject: Case Summary – 26-Month Security Screening Delay (OINP Masters Stream)**

I am an applicant under the **Ontario Masters Graduate Stream (OINP)**. Having lived and studied in Canada since my **senior year of high school**, I have established my professional life in **Toronto**. However, my permanent residence application has been stalled in **Security Screening** for **over two and a half years**, without any meaningful updates.

---

## Case Timeline

**March 2023**
Entered the **OINP pool** and received a provincial nomination.

**August 2023**
Submitted my **federal permanent residence application**.

**December 2023**
Received the file number (FN). **Medical examinations and biometrics** were completed.

**January 2024**
I was requested to submit a **CV**, after which my application entered **Security Screening**.

**March 2026 (Present)**
There has been **zero progress or communication for 26 consecutive months**.

---

## Concrete Negative Impacts

### Career Disadvantage

My **3-year Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP)** has already expired, forcing me to rely on a **Bridging Open Work Permit (BOWP)**.

Without permanent resident status, my career progression faces a **glass ceiling**. Many **senior roles, leadership positions, and promotions** are effectively closed to candidates who do not yet hold PR status.

---

### Financial Penalty (NRST Rebate)

I closed on a **pre-construction property in late 2025**. Because my PR application remains delayed, I encountered significant complications during the **title transfer process**.

More critically, I am now **disqualified from the Non-Resident Speculation Tax (NRST) rebate**, because the government has not granted me PR within the **18-month eligibility window**.

In effect, I am being **financially penalized for an administrative delay entirely beyond my control**.

---

## My Position

I understand the necessity of **security protocols**.

However, a **two-year black hole** for an applicant who has lived in Canada since high school is not a matter of due diligence. It reflects **administrative negligence**.

The lack of coordination and accountability between **CSIS, CBSA, and IRCC** is deeply troubling.

---

## What I Demand

**Transparency**
Applicants deserve clear and real-time updates regarding the status of their security screenings.

**Accountability**
There should be a **mandatory processing timeframe** for applicants who have no sensitive backgrounds.

**Fairness**
Policies should be adjusted to ensure applicants are **not financially penalized** because of government-induced delays (for example, eligibility for **NRST rebates**).

---

## Closing

Thank you again for standing up for applicants who are experiencing prolonged security screening delays.

It is encouraging to see our community come together to **demand transparency and fairness**. I hope that my story can add weight to our collective voice.
