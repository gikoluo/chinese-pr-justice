# 68 Months of Waiting: A Life Put on Hold by Administrative Errors and Security Screening

I submitted my permanent residence application through the **Quebec Skilled Worker Program** in **July 2020**. Now, after **68 months of waiting**, my life remains on hold due to the interminable immigration process and prolonged security screening.

## Phase One: 2020 – 2022

The first phase of my application lasted from **2020 to the end of 2022**.

During this period, we responded promptly to every request from IRCC. We completed our **medical examinations on time** and submitted all required documents without delay. Although two years had already exceeded the typical processing time for PR applications under the Quebec program, I did not raise concerns at the time, considering the extraordinary circumstances caused by the **COVID-19 pandemic**.

However, when the estimated completion date shown on the IRCC website passed without any news, I contacted IRCC by phone for the first time.

To my shock, I was informed that **my husband’s documents had been mistakenly filed under a separate permanent residence application as an individual applicant**, even though he should have been included as the **secondary applicant in my application**.

## Phase Two: 2022 – 2023

This discovery marked the beginning of the **second phase** of our immigration process.

From **late 2022 to early 2023**, IRCC required my husband to **resubmit all documents and pay the application fees again**. This effectively erased the entire waiting period we had already endured.

Nearly **two additional years of waiting became invalid** because of this administrative error.

We spent almost another year fulfilling IRCC’s new requirements, submitting documents and complying with repeated requests.

## Phase Three: 2023 – Present

After **December 2023**, our application entered its **third phase: endless waiting**.

Since then, we have received **no meaningful communication** from IRCC.

Whether through **webforms or phone calls**, the only response we consistently receive is:

> “Please be patient.”

Over the past six years, I have submitted **six ATIP requests**. Only about **half were answered on time**. One response arrived **nine months late**, and my **two most recent requests** (submitted in **September 2025** and **February 2026**) have **not received any response at all**.

## The Human Cost of Six Years

Two years of **invalid waiting caused by administrative error**, combined with **more than two years of security screening**, have consumed most of the timeline of my application.

During this time:

* Because of the pandemic and the repeated need to **renew our temporary status**, we have **not been able to visit our parents for five years**.
* We were unable to attend **my grandmother’s funeral**.
* Our **housing plans** have been postponed indefinitely.
* Our **professional development** has been interrupted.
* Several **career advancement opportunities** have passed us by.

Despite our busy daily work routines, we remain suspended in uncertainty.

Every day we wait for a decision that should normally have been made **within one to two years**. We keep hoping that once the result finally arrives, we can begin the next chapter of our lives.

Instead, it feels as if we are **athletes held behind the starting line**, waiting for a race that never begins, while other applicants who submitted their applications around the same time have already moved far ahead.

## A Call for Fairness

I am speaking out now simply to ask for **fair and equal treatment**.

During our years in Canada, we have:

* Obeyed all laws
* Participated actively in community life
* Devoted ourselves to our work
* Paid **school taxes, property taxes, and income taxes**

We believe in Canada’s values and hope to see an immigration system that is **more transparent, reasonable, and fair**, with clearly defined timelines.

After **68 months of waiting**, that is all we ask.
