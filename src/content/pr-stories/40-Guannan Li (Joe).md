**Guannan Li (Joe)**
Licensed Traditional Chinese Medicine Practitioner & Acupuncturist
Markham, Toronto

Hello everyone,

My name is **Guannan Li (Joe)**, and I am a licensed **Traditional Chinese Medicine practitioner and acupuncturist** practicing independently in **Markham, Toronto**.

In **2019**, I left a stable job in my home country to pursue education in Canada. After graduating, I continued to work hard and officially obtained my professional license to practice in **2023**.

Over the past **seven years**, I have built my life here step by step—studying and working legally, treating local patients in my clinic every day, paying taxes, and truly making Canada my home.

In **August 2024**, I submitted my permanent residence application full of hope. However, since **November 2024**, my file has been placed into the black box of so-called **“security screening.”** A full **19 months** have passed without a single explanation or update.

This completely **non-transparent delay** is tearing my family apart and strangling my professional future.

Because of the prolonged uncertainty surrounding our status, my **wife lost her healthcare coverage** in Canada and was forced to **return to China alone during her pregnancy**, leaving us separated for nearly a year.

Furthermore, because she had to leave Canada, she also lost her job **teaching Chinese to local children**. In order to remain compliant with immigration requirements while waiting, she even had to **miss her grandfather’s funeral**.

Now, in order to **fly back this July to be with her for the delivery of our child**, I am facing the devastating reality of **interrupting my medical practice** and leaving my local patients behind. In many ways, this feels like a **soft deportation of a healthcare worker** who simply wants to continue serving people in Canada.

At the same time, my **alma mater, Humber Polytechnic**, has just received approval for **Ontario’s first Bachelor of Science in Traditional Chinese Medicine**. The program will open a **bridge pathway for early graduates like us in January 2027**.

This should be an incredible opportunity for me to **upgrade my medical training and better serve the community**.

However, if IRCC’s unreasonable delay leaves me without permanent resident status by that time, I will be forced to pay **international student tuition fees again**. As a professional who has already been **working and paying taxes in Canada for years**, this is not only unreasonable—it would create an overwhelming financial burden.

We are **not just cold file numbers**.

We are real families who are **actively contributing to Canadian society**.

Today we are asking IRCC for **basic fairness**:
stop the **black-box process**, and provide a **transparent and reasonable processing timeline** for those whose lives are being held in indefinite uncertainty.
