I am DQQ, a Project Engineer working for a global group based in Winnipeg, Manitoba. I submitted my permanent residence (PR) application in March 2024 through the Manitoba Provincial Nominee Program (MPNP). Since August 2024, my application has been under security screening, with no explanation and no timeline for when it might conclude.

## Family Separation Across Three Locations

My spouse and I have already been living apart for five years while I have worked and built our life in Canada. Rather than bringing our family back together, the prolonged security screening has only added to our separation.

Our child completed grades 9 through 12 in Canada and, in September 2025, began a Health Science university program. Because of where our family members currently need to be — for work, for school, and for the immigration process itself — our family of three now lives in three separate locations. Maintaining three households at once has placed a heavy financial burden on us and taken a real emotional toll on every member of our family.

## Educational and Financial Hardship

Without PR status, my child is charged international student tuition — $35,000 to $40,000 more per year than a domestic student would pay — bringing the total annual cost to approximately $70,000 to $80,000. The tuition deadline for the upcoming academic year is approaching, and this cost has become unaffordable for our family.

We have followed every step of the process honestly and in good faith. We passed eligibility, and all that remains is a security screening that has already lasted nearly two years with no update.

## What We Want the Government to Know

Security screening should not come at the cost of a family's ability to stay together or a child's ability to continue their education without crushing financial strain. We respectfully ask Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) to:

- Provide a clear timeline for completing security screening on cases like mine that have already been pending for close to two years.
- Recognize the compounding hardship faced by families who are separated across multiple locations while waiting.
- Consider humanitarian and financial hardship — including international tuition burdens on children already in Canada — when prioritizing delayed cases.

Our family has done everything right. We simply ask for a fair and timely resolution so we can finally live together again.

DQQ (大奇奇)
