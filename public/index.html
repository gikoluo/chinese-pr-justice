Declaration of Justice

We Stand for Fair Immigration Processing

We are Chinese immigrants, students, workers, and families who have followed every rule, paid every fee, and waited patiently for our Permanent Residence applications to be processed. Yet we face unprecedented delays of 3-5+ years due to prolonged security screening.

We Declare That:

  • Equality: We deserve the same processing standards and timelines as applicants from other countries. Current delays disproportionately affect Chinese applicants.
  • Transparency: We have the right to know why security screening takes 3-5+ years when all admissibility requirements are already met. We deserve clear explanations, not indefinite silence.
  • Fairness: Security screening should not be used as a tool for discrimination. Thorough security checks are reasonable; indefinite delays without explanation are not.
  • Accountability: IRCC's pattern of missed deadlines and broken promises shows systematic failure. We demand accountability for these delays that destroy lives and futures.
  • Human Dignity: We are not case numbers. We are human beings with families, careers, children, and dreams. Our lives are on hold while bureaucracy fails us.

The Human Cost

These delays have severe consequences:

  • Children lose eligibility for domestic tuition ($130,000-200,000 per child)
  • Families cannot reunite with elderly parents
  • Careers are stalled and opportunities lost
  • Mental health suffers from years of uncertainty
  • Work permits expire, forcing families to leave Canada
  • Repeated medical exams and police certificates cost thousands

We have waited long enough. We demand action now.

What We Are Calling For

We call on the Canadian Government, Immigration Minister, and IRCC to take immediate action:

1

Immediate Processing

Complete all security screenings for cases pending over 2 years within 90 days. No more indefinite delays.

2

Equal Treatment

Ensure Chinese applicants receive the same processing standards and timelines as applicants from other countries. End discriminatory practices.

3

Transparency & Communication

Provide clear explanations for security screening delays beyond 6 months. Give applicants specific information about their cases, not generic responses.

4

Accountability for Delays

Hold IRCC accountable for missed deadlines and processing failures. Establish enforceable timelines with consequences for non-compliance.

5

Humanitarian Consideration

Implement expedited processing for humanitarian cases: families with children in school, expired work permits, elderly parents, medical emergencies.

6

Temporary Measures

Grant domestic tuition eligibility and temporary PR benefits to applicants who have passed all other requirements while security screening completes.

7

Mandamus Reform

Support judicial remedies for unreasonable delays. Stop using "national security" as blanket justification to dismiss legitimate Mandamus applications.

8

Public Apology

Minister of Immigration must publicly acknowledge the disproportionate impact on Chinese applicants and commit to concrete solutions with timelines.

Chunhui Luo's Story

BC PNP Applicant ‚Ä¢ 3.5+ Years Waiting

I applied for PR through BC PNP over 3 years ago. Everything passed - eligibility, criminality, medical. Only security screening remains. My file has a pattern of missed deadlines documented in my CBSA notes. Every "due date" IRCC sets gets ignored.

The impact on my family: My son is in Grade 11, applying to universities next year. Without PR, he'll pay international tuition - $40,000-60,000 per year instead of $8,000-15,000. That's a $130,000-180,000 difference for his degree.

UBC just repealed domestic tuition for work permit holders in May 2025. Our work permits don't help anymore. PR is the ONLY solution.

My work permit expires October 2026. If PR isn't approved, my entire family may be forced to leave Canada - the place my son calls home, where he's built his life.

"We followed every rule. We did everything right. Yet we're treated like we don't deserve basic fairness. My son's future is being destroyed by bureaucratic delays."
