**Anonymous Applicant**

# A Plea for Procedural Fairness: The Human Cost of IRCC’s “Security Screening” Black Box

**To the Honourable Minister of Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship,**

Our family’s journey toward becoming Canadians began in **late 2023**. By **January 2024**, our application for permanent residence entered the **Security Screening** phase. Since then, for **over 25 months**, we have lived in complete administrative silence.

There have been **no updates, no requests for information, and no communication**—only the crushing weight of a process that has effectively placed our lives on indefinite hold.

This is not merely a delay in statistics. It is the **systematic erosion of a family’s future**.

Our story began in the **spring of 2022** during the severe lockdowns in Shanghai. During that difficult period, we looked toward Canada as a **beacon of stability, human rights, and the rule of law**. We chose Canada because we believed in its values of **fairness and inclusion**.

To reach this dream, we did everything that was asked of us.

The greatest sacrifice was made by **my wife**.

While **pregnant**, she spent long days commuting across Shanghai on crowded subways, studying English from her exam preparation books. Even when she was being wheeled into the **delivery room**, she refused to put down her vocabulary book. She told me that focusing on learning English helped her endure the physical pain of labor. For her, the **IELTS score** was not just an immigration requirement—it was the **first gift she wanted to give our newborn child**.

When pandemic restrictions suddenly lifted in a chaotic wave, our entire **five-member household fell ill with high fevers**. With medicine in short supply, we had to rely on a few tablets of **paracetamol mailed from friends in Beijing**.

Despite postpartum exhaustion and illness, my wife **never stopped studying**.

In **May 2023**, while still breastfeeding our child, she walked into the IELTS exam center carrying a **breast pump in her bag**, using the short breaks between exam modules to manage her physical pain. I remember kissing her forehead outside the exam gates, moved to tears by her determination.

We did our part.

We proved our commitment.

But the **security screening process** has turned that commitment into a nightmare.

For more than **two years**, our family has lived in a state of **suspended animation**. We cannot change jobs for fear of disrupting the immigration process. We cannot plan for our child’s education. We cannot move forward with our lives.

Our initial excitement about Canada has slowly been replaced by the realization that we are trapped in an **opaque and unaccountable system**—one that appears to affect **Chinese applicants disproportionately**.

We understand that **national security is important**.

But **security must not become a justification for administrative paralysis**.

Our requests are simple and reasonable:

### 1. A Definitive Timeline

Administrative processes should not become **life sentences**. Every applicant deserves to know when a decision will be made.

### 2. Procedural Transparency

Security screening must not become a **black box used for silent rejection or profiling**. Applicants deserve to know the status of their files and the reasons for extraordinary delays.

Time is the most precious resource any person possesses.

We have offered our **skills, loyalty, and resilience** to Canada. In return, we ask only for the value Canada has long claimed to uphold: **procedural fairness**.

Please do not allow our lives to remain trapped inside a bureaucratic black box.

We are ready to contribute to Canada’s future.

We ask only for the opportunity to begin.

**Respectfully,**

On behalf of a family waiting for justice.
