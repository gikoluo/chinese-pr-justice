**Anonymous Applicant**

My partner and I applied for permanent residence through **BC PNP combined with Express Entry**. We received our **Acknowledgment of Receipt (AOR) in May 2023** and our file entered **security screening in July 2023**. So far, it has been **969 days**.

I filed a **Mandamus application by myself in February 2025**, but it was dismissed by the court at the end of last year.

This prolonged waiting has been incredibly stressful for us. Our **job opportunities have been limited** due to the delay, and more and more companies are now requiring **PR status** for positions. We are constantly worried about our eligibility to **stay and work in Canada**.

The uncertainty has created serious difficulties in our daily lives and has had a **significant impact on our mental health**. We also face constant pressure from our parents and others who do not understand why we have spent so much money studying and living here for nearly **10 years**, yet still do not have a stable status in Canada.

Because of this uncertainty, we cannot make normal life decisions. We hesitate to **buy a home** due to the overseas buyers tax, and we are reluctant to **move to a new place** because we do not know whether we will be able to stay or work legally in Canada in the future.

Now we are spending even more **time and money** preparing **another Mandamus application with the assistance of a professional lawyer**, simply to ask the government to make a decision.

What makes this situation even more difficult is seeing that many of my **colleagues of other nationalities receive their PR quickly**. Some studied the **same major** as I did at university, and some even have **multiple dependents in their applications**. Yet none of them have been stuck in security screening for nearly as long as we have.

Among the people I know, **only those who share my nationality are experiencing these extreme delays**.

Every time a recruiter contacts me and then ends the conversation because of my **lack of PR status**, the situation feels incredibly unfair.

I want the government to understand that **we are not harmful to this country**. We simply want the opportunity to **live and work here**, and to contribute our skills and knowledge to Canadian industries.

We should not be treated like prisoners.

I understand that the field I studied may be considered sensitive. However, both my partner and I **came to Canada as 18-year-old students**, and we have **no criminal record in any country**. I cannot see any reason why a security check should take this long.
