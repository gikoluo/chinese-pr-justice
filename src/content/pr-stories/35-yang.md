**Dear Officer,**

I am writing to share the impact that prolonged and unexplained immigration security screening delays have had on my family.

I never imagined that my family would also need to go through security screening. The most difficult part of this situation is the feeling of helplessness—there is literally nothing we can do to accelerate the process.

My parents previously held visitor visas before their passports expired. I encouraged them to apply for a Super Visa so they could spend extended time with me in Canada. I prepared all of the application materials myself.

During their last visit, we went to house showings together. I finally purchased my own home so that my parents could live with me comfortably instead of staying in a rented house. We planned everything together and I was incredibly excited about the future.

I submitted the Super Visa application in **June last year**. When I realized that the processing time had already exceeded the posted **60-day timeline**, I requested records from **IRCC and CBSA**. Through those disclosures, I learned that my father had been placed into **security screening**, while my mother had been flagged for a **possible interview**. Since then, there has been **no progress**.

For a long time I blamed myself for choosing to apply for the Super Visa and for not hiring an agency to prepare the materials. I keep looking at the furniture in my new home that remains unpacked, feeling sad about the family time that may no longer happen for at least another two years. I stopped making plans and try to pretend everything is normal when I talk with my family.

My family is not wealthy. I saved for many years to purchase this home so that my parents could finally live with me. I am here alone, and sometimes I wake up in the middle of the night from dreams where both of them are already here in the house with me.

My father has **never served in the military or the government**, and I have no idea why he was selected for security screening. The **lack of transparency** and not knowing the reason behind this decision has been extremely painful.

Compared to others, my case has been pending for **nine months**, which may seem short. But after reading many similar stories online, I have seen people who have been waiting since **2023 or even earlier**, and many of them are still stuck in security screening today. Knowing this makes the situation even more frightening because I do not know when my turn will come if those cases have not yet been resolved.

I believe that **IRCC, CBSA, and CSIS should respect reasonable timelines and treat applicants seriously and fairly**. We are residents in Canada and contribute to society through taxes and work. It is difficult to understand how families can be randomly flagged for years-long security checks without explanation.

My parents are already **over 60 years old**. Every year lost to these unexplained delays is time that we will never get back together. We have also spent significant money on **travel, documentation, and medical examinations**, many of which expire because of the delays.

I sincerely hope that this event can become a starting point for all of us to be heard, and that we can stand together until families like ours are treated with the respect and fairness we deserve.

Unfortunately I do not have a car and cannot attend in person. Although it is a small amount, I hope my donation can contribute in some way to this effort.

Thank you again for organizing this.

**Best regards,**
Yang
