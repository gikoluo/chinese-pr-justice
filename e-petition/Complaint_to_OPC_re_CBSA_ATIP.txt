4. GROUNDS FOR COMPLAINT

Under section 14 of the Privacy Act, a government institution must respond to a
personal information request within 30 days of receipt. The institution may extend
this period under section 15, but ONLY if it notifies the requester in writing
within the initial 30-day period, stating the reason for the extension and the
new expected completion date.

CBSA has done neither:
  (a) No records or partial records have been provided;
  (b) No acknowledgment of processing has been communicated;
  (c) No notice of extension under section 15 has been sent;
  (d) No communication of any kind has been received from CBSA.

This constitutes a deemed refusal under the Privacy Act.


5. IMPACT OF NON-COMPLIANCE

The requested records are urgently needed because:

  (a) My PR application has been in security screening for over 40 months —
      far beyond any published service standard;

  (b) CSIS confirmed completion of its screening phase on July 16, 2025, meaning
      CBSA has held my file for over 7 months with no explanation;

  (c) My work permit and my son's study permit expire in October 2026;

  (d) My son will apply to Canadian universities in Fall 2026 for Fall 2027
      admission. Without PR, he faces international student tuition
      ($40,000-65,000/year) versus domestic tuition ($7,000-15,000/year) —
      a difference of $130,000-200,000 over four years;

  (e) Without these records, I cannot understand what CBSA is doing with my file,
      what remains outstanding, or make informed decisions about my family's future;

  (f) The delay in responding to this ATIP request compounds an already
      unacceptable 40-month processing delay, further eroding my ability to
      plan and protect my family's interests.


6. RELIEF REQUESTED

I respectfully request that the Privacy Commissioner:

  (a) Investigate CBSA's failure to comply with the 30-day statutory deadline;

  (b) Order CBSA to process and release my records immediately;

  (c) Determine whether CBSA's failure to respond constitutes a systemic issue
      affecting other applicants in similar security screening situations;

  (d) Report findings and recommendations to CBSA regarding its ATIP compliance
      obligations.
